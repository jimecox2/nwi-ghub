# Business Case — Fort Frances & Area Wireless Broadband Network

**Prepared for:** Jam21.net (in coordination with Rainy River Futures Development Corporation)
**Prepared by:** Northern Wireless Inc. (NWI)
**Date:** June 19, 2026
**Reference:** NWI-BC-FF-2026 · Rev 0

---

## 1. Purpose

This business case supports Jam21.net's decision to build a fixed-wireless broadband
network serving Fort Frances and the surrounding area. It summarizes the recommended
solution, the capital cost to build it, and the operating model that sustains it, so
that Jam21.net can proceed to a supply-and-install contract with NWI.

## 2. Opportunity

Fort Frances and the neighbouring communities have limited affordable broadband. A
fixed-wireless network lets Jam21.net deliver high-speed internet to schools,
businesses and residents at a fraction of the cost of wired build-out, with fast
deployment and the ability to grow capacity as demand grows.

## 3. Recommended Solution

A complete turnkey network supplied, installed and commissioned by NWI:

- **Backbone:** 5.8 GHz point-to-point / point-to-multipoint links from a central tower.
- **Last mile:** 802.11-based customer premise equipment (CPE) at each subscriber.
- **Capacity:** designed for **60 Mbps**, against an estimated 5-year area demand of
  **50 Mbps** — headroom to extend service to nearby towns.
- **Coverage:** up to **8 break-out locations** feeding local last-mile cells.
- **Core services:** bandwidth-control, primary/secondary servers and T1 hand-off,
  ready to integrate with Jam21.net's existing ISP (RADIUS, email, web).

Jam21.net owns and operates all equipment on completion. NWI trains Jam21.net staff to
run and maintain the network.

## 4. Capital Investment (Supply & Install)

One-time turnkey cost to build the network, covering 100 initial subscriber
connections:

| Work package | Scope | Amount (CAD) |
|---|---|---:|
| Engineering & Admin | Site study, design, drawings, technical manual, procurement | $10,480 |
| Tower | Supply, installation, power & lighting | $17,000 |
| Tower POP (head-end) | Antennas, amplifiers, AP enclosure, cabling, install | $5,500 |
| Wireless Backhaul | Backhaul radios, cabling, install both ends | $5,500 |
| Core Wired Network | Bandwidth control, switch, primary/secondary servers, T1/router | $19,500 |

| **Total supply & install** | | **$97,500** |

Pricing is firm and itemized; the full line-item breakdown carries into the supply
contract. Government rural-broadband subsidies, where secured, offset part of this
capital cost.

## 5. Operating Model (Monthly)

The network is designed to **break even at launch** on a modest anchor base, then
generate surplus as subscribers are added.

**Monthly operating costs**

| Item | Monthly (CAD) |
|---|---:|
| T1 backhaul (internet transit) | $2,700 |
| Network operations (part-time, ~4 hrs/day @ $15/hr) | $1,600 |
| **Total** | **$4,300** |

**Monthly recurring revenue at launch**

| Segment | Accounts | $/mo each | Monthly (CAD) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Schools (256 kbps) | 2 | $300 | $600 |
| Business | 10 | $100 | $1,000 |
| Residential | 20 | $45 | $900 |
| Anchor tenant — Wasaya | 1 | $800 | $800 |
| Anchor tenant — Mishkeegogamang | 1 | $1,000 | $1,000 |
| **Total** | | | **$4,300** |

**Net at launch: $0/month (break-even).** Fixed costs (T1 + part-time ops) do not rise
materially with new subscribers, so most revenue beyond the launch base is surplus.

## 6. Growth & Payback

Each additional subscriber is largely margin once the fixed base is covered:

- **+40 residential** (@ $45) and **+10 business** (@ $100) in Year 2 ≈ **+$2,800/month**
  (~$33,600/year) surplus.
- At that growth, operating surplus repays the $97,500 capital investment in roughly
  **3 years** on subscriber revenue alone — sooner if broadband subsidies offset capital.

The model scales: the 60 Mbps design and 8 break-out sites allow expansion into
adjacent communities without re-engineering the core.

## 7. Funding

- **Capital:** Jam21.net, coordinated financially through Rainy River Futures
  Development Corporation, with government rural-broadband programs as available.
- **Operating:** self-funding from subscriber revenue from launch.
- **Tower rental** and **bandwidth resale** remain Jam21.net's responsibility; NWI
  supplies, installs, tests and hands over the network.

## 8. Key Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Backbone link outage affects multiple cells | On-hand spares and certified climbers; proven 5.8 GHz radios |
| Subscriber uptake slower than plan | Break-even at a small anchor base; low fixed cost to hold |
| Capacity growth outpaces design | 60 Mbps design vs. 50 Mbps 5-year demand; bandwidth control per user |
| Schedule slippage across parties | Single master schedule with milestone dates, agreed before execution |

## 9. Recommendation & Next Steps

The solution is technically sound, break-even from launch, and scalable. **NWI
recommends Jam21.net proceed.**
